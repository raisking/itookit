IT Strategy Last updated: 2020-06-29

IT Strategy- can be a long-term and short-term plan for achieving a goal in the fast-changing technology field. For example, a small company using a WordPress platform to host their online store, but it is feasible to use WordPress when the business grows bigger in 3-5 years.

Business Strategy

is planning and decision management makes in an organization to reach its goal and be competitive in the market at the same time.

Business-IT Strategy Alignment

“Business-IT alignment is a process in which a business organization uses information technology (IT) to achieve business objectives - typically improved financial performance or marketplace competitiveness.” In a simple term, Information technology is used as tools to fix, manage, and to achieve the goal.

Business Model

“A business model describes the rationale of how an organization creates, delivers, and captures value, in economic, social, cultural or other contexts.” Starting any new business should have a business model to be successful. The business model includes design a product and raw materials, marketing, and sales to price and payment methods.

Value Creation

“is performance of actions that increase the worth of goods, services, or even a business.”

Digital Strategy

is a part of business strategy. In digital strategy, the use of social media platforms, data modeling, and data analysis are used to market and target the right consumers.

Value Chain

“ is a business model that describes the full range of activities needed to create a product or service.” The activities process include from the start to finish and provide a visual model of business to align with the competitors.

SWOT analysis

“ is a strategic planning technique used to help a person or organization identify strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats related to business competition or project planning.”

IT Policy

IT Policy- “the organization defines rules, regulations, and guidelines for proper usage and maintenance of these technological assets to ensure their ethical and acceptable use and assure health, safety and security of data, products, facilities as well as the people using them.”

Access control policy

making sure the users are who they say they are and making sure the authorized users are not allowed to access the system.

User Account policy

“document which outlines the requirements for requesting and maintaining an account on computer systems or networks, typically within an organization”

Industrial espionage

“industrial espionage is the illegal and unethical theft of business trade secrets for use by a competitor to achieve a competitive advantage. Industrial espionage is conducted by companies for commercial purposes rather than governments for national security purposes”

Cybercrime

any crime done using computers such as online fraud, intellectual property or stealing identities.

IT Governance

IT Governance- “ is a process used to monitor and control key information technology capability decisions - in an attempt - to ensure the delivery of value to key stakeholders in an organization. ”

Governance

“Establishment of policies, and continuous monitoring of their proper implementation, by the members of the governing body of an organization.”

Governance, Risk Management, and Compliance (GRC)

“refers to a strategy for managing an organization's overall governance, enterprise risk management, and compliance with regulations.”

COBIT

is guidance to organize the activities that are related to IT within the business activities properly and reliably. COBIT process model helps enterprises understand the nature of all these activities that relate to IT and can be reliably performed.

Balanced scorecard

“strategic planning and performance management framework used by business, government, and non-profits to align day-to-day activities with enterprise vision, mission, and values.”

IT management

“the process of overseeing all matters related to information technology operations and resources within an IT organization.”

IT Dictionry

IT Terminology - commonly used terminology, acronym and abbreviation in the Information Technology

IT Terminology

Access point: A device that allows wireless-equipped computers and other devices to communicate with a wired network.

Authentication: the process of identiying yourself and the verification that you are who you say you are.

Authorization: the process of granting or denying a user access to network resources.

AWS: Amazon Web Service is a subsidary of Amazon that provide cloud computing service to businesses and individuals.

API: An application programming interface is a computing interface that defines interactions between multiple software intermediaries.

Business Continuity: Activity performed by an organization to ensure that critical business functions will be available to customers, suppliers, regulators, and other entities that must have access to those functions.

Cache: A set of files saved on your hard disk that help your browser display pages you have already visited more quickly. It displays the files from your hard disk instead of the web.

Content Management: Content management is a collection of tools, software and processes that allow you to collect, manage and publish information on any medium.

Cookie: A small piece of information you may be asked to accept when connecting to certain servers via a web browser.

Customer Relationship Management: Customer relationship management refers to a set of processes, tools, technologies and software that help businesses manage their relationships with their customers.

Cyber Physical Attacks: A security breach in cyberspace that impacts on the physical environment.

Data center: Facility used to house computer systems and associated components, such as telecommunications and storage systems.

DDoS Attacks: A distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attack is a malicious attempt to disrupt normal traffic to a web property.

Encryption: The manipulation of data to prevent accurate interpretation by all but those for whom the data is intended.

IaaS: Infrastructure as a Service; In the most basic cloud-service model, providers of IaaS offer computers – physical or (more often) virtual machines – and other resources.

Information Security Policy: An information security policy is a set of rules that guide individuals who work with IT assets.

Internet of Things (IoT): The Internet of Things, or IoT, refers to the billions of physical devices around the world that are now connected to the internet, all collecting and sharing data.

Protocol – TCP, UDP, ICMP, etc.: Protocols are different ways of communicating over the Internet.

Risk Management: Risk management is the application of principles to manage the risks in a business that come with the ownership, involvement, operation, influence, adoption and use of IT in an organization.

SMPT: Simple Mail Transfer Protocol is an Internet standard for e-mail transmission.

VoIP: Voice over Internet Protocol; a means of using the Internet as the transmission medium for phone calls.

WAN: A wide area network is a larger network that covers a wider area.

WLAN: Wireless Local Area Network; the computers and devices that make up a wireless network.
